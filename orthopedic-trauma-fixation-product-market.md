<p><ul><li>Internal Fixation Groups Plates</li><li>Internal Fixation Screws</li><li>Internal Fixation Nail</li><li>Internal Fixation Others</li><li>External Fixation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The orthopedic trauma fixation product market consists of various types aimed at stabilizing fractures. Internal fixation includes plates, screws, nails, and other devices that are surgically placed within the body to hold bones in alignment. Plates provide surface support, screws secure fragments, and nails align long bone fractures. On the other hand, external fixation involves devices applied outside the body, connecting to the skeleton through pins, offering stability while allowing for wound care and easier access during healing.</p></p>
